Subject: Debt V2.2 — Type-Specific Intake and Lifecycle Engine

Debt V2.2 is implemented and packaged for review.

What changed
- Expanded Debt intake so the guided flow now adapts more directly by debt type instead of presenting one generic setup sequence.
- Preserved normalization into one shared Debt account model while collecting type-specific fields for mortgage, student loan, auto loan, other loan, line of credit, financed purchase, credit card, and BNPL cases.
- Kept standing state separate from arrangement overlays and exposed lifecycle detail through the account detail surfaces already present in Debt.
- Hardened continuity handling so predecessor obligations are suppressed when a confirmed successor account fully takes over the transferred balance, preventing double-counting in downstream projected rows.
- Exposed lifecycle-rule assumptions in Debt settings so grace windows, delinquency thresholds, partial-payment allocation behavior, and cure treatment stay inspectable.
- Added scenario-based lifecycle tests covering the nine required V2.2 cases.

Type-specific intake
- DebtSection now varies labels, helper text, and field groups by debt type.
- Mortgage, student loan, auto loan, line of credit, financed purchase, BNPL, and installment-style loans now surface more relevant fields by default.
- Advanced lifecycle sections remain progressive-disclosure based rather than front-loading every field.

Debt-type behavior rules
- Debt math still normalizes into one engine.
- Type-specific behavior is attached through stored fields, effective term versions, overlays, lifecycle events, and continuity links rather than branching the whole downstream model.

Standing state
- Standing state remains derived and explainable from account truth, bills, overlays, and lifecycle events.
- Severe states, grace-window handling, late/delinquent progression, cure amount, and next escalation remain part of the lifecycle snapshot.

Arrangement overlays
- Arrangement overlays remain first-class and distinct from standing state.
- Hardship, deferment, forbearance, dispute, settlement, collector plan, and temporary-skip overlays continue to flow into lifecycle detail and downstream facts.

Term versioning
- Effective-dated term versions remain active in the debt engine.
- V2.2 tests now cover promo expiry and capitalization scenarios so forward math reflects term changes from the right date onward.

Lifecycle events and timeline
- Lifecycle events remain first-class and inspectable.
- The account detail timeline continues to expose event type, date, source, source quality, and correction-related metadata.
- New test coverage now exercises failed payments, reversed payments, capitalization, collections confirmation, and dispute coexistence.

Continuity handling
- Balance transfer, refinance, consolidation, servicer transfer, debt sale, and account replacement continuity types continue to be modeled.
- Downstream projection now suppresses predecessor schedule rows when a confirmed successor account fully assumes the transferred balance.

Source-quality and trust rules
- Source quality remains visible in overlays, term versions, lifecycle events, conflict handling, and downstream facts.
- Manual-confirmed collections state is now covered in scenario tests.

Duplicate and correction protection
- Existing event normalization and duplicate suppression remain in place.
- Reversal and failed-payment scenarios are now covered explicitly in tests so incorrect satisfaction effects are removed and conflicts remain visible.

Intentionally deferred
- No new Atlas-specific interpretation layer was added.
- No new Bills ownership logic was added beyond bounded downstream projection safeguards.
- No redesign of Overview or Reporting was introduced.
