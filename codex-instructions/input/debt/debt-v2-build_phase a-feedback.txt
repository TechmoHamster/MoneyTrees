DEBT TEAM — V2A REVIEW FEEDBACK (CONCERNS)

V2A is approved. The core math foundation, trust system, and UI integration are solid.

The following items are NOT blockers, but must be acknowledged and considered moving into V2B:

--------------------------------------------------
1. TRUST-STATE PRECISION IS TOO COARSE
--------------------------------------------------
Current trust labeling (Manual, Custom, Estimated, etc.) is directionally correct, but lacks nuance.

Examples:
- Payment amount trust defaults heavily to "Manual" or "Custom"
- No distinction between:
  - verified amortized loan payment
  - user-entered estimate
  - derived vs inferred values

Expectation:
V2B should begin refining trust-state granularity where possible, especially for:
- payment amount reliability
- interest modeling confidence
- projection accuracy tiers

--------------------------------------------------
2. EXTRA-PAYMENT SCENARIO LABELING IS OVER-SIMPLIFIED
--------------------------------------------------
All extra-payment scenarios are labeled as "Custom" regardless of underlying model quality.

Issue:
- A no-interest fixed loan with deterministic math is still labeled "Custom"
- This hides when a scenario is actually mathematically exact vs user-adjusted

Expectation:
Future phases should differentiate:
- "Exact (adjusted)"
- "Estimated (adjusted)"
- vs purely "Custom"

--------------------------------------------------
3. CREDIT CARD MODELING IS CORRECTLY LIMITED — BUT THIS IS THE BIG RISK AREA
--------------------------------------------------
Credit card projections are intentionally marked as "Limited" in V2A.

This is correct.

However:
Credit card minimum payment behavior is highly variable and issuer-specific:
- % of balance (1–4%)
- % + interest
- floor amounts
- past-due carryover rules  [oai_citation:0‡NerdWallet](https://www.nerdwallet.com/credit-cards/learn/credit-card-issuer-minimum-payment?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Also:
Minimum payments often barely reduce principal and can extend payoff timelines decades  [oai_citation:1‡OptaRev Debt Calculator](https://www.credittech.finance/learn/debt-payoff-strategies/minimum-payment-trap?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Implication:
V2B is the most critical phase of the entire Debt system.

Expectation:
- Do NOT approximate minimum payment logic generically
- Build a configurable rule system (issuer-based or user-defined)
- Avoid false precision at all costs

--------------------------------------------------
4. TEST COVERAGE IS STILL LIGHT
--------------------------------------------------
Tests exist and pass (good improvement), but coverage is narrow.

Missing:
- edge cases (zero/negative amortization boundaries)
- cadence variations (weekly/biweekly vs monthly)
- malformed input scenarios
- long-tail projection stability

Expectation:
V2B should expand test coverage alongside new logic, especially for:
- credit card rules
- edge-case projections
- failure states

--------------------------------------------------
5. PROJECTION MODEL ASSUMPTIONS ARE STATIC
--------------------------------------------------
Current projection assumes:
- constant payment
- stable APR
- no behavioral change

This is acceptable for V2A.

But:
Real-world behavior (especially credit cards) is dynamic and non-linear:
- minimum payments change as balance changes
- interest dominates early payments  [oai_citation:2‡OptaRev Debt Calculator](https://www.credittech.finance/learn/debt-payoff-strategies/minimum-payment-trap?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Expectation:
V2B should begin introducing:
- dynamic payment behavior (where applicable)
- rule-driven recalculation cycles
- clearer separation between "simulation" vs "static projection"

--------------------------------------------------
SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------
V2A is approved.

Key takeaway:
- The math engine is now strong
- The trust system is correctly introduced
- The UI surfaces the right level of detail

BUT:
V2B is where correctness either holds or breaks — especially for credit cards.

Proceed carefully, prioritize accuracy over completeness, and maintain strict trust labeling discipline.
