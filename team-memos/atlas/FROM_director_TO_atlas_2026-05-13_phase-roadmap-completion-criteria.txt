DIRECTOR MEMO
FROM: director
TO: atlas
DATE: 2026-05-13
TOPIC: Phase Roadmap Completion Criteria — Phases 1-5

==================================================
1. PURPOSE
==================================================

This memo extends the new product directive into a phased roadmap.

MoneyTrees is now operating toward a five-phase buildout:

Phase 1 — Financial Survival + Core Usability
Phase 2 — Full Budgeting Platform
Phase 3 — Financial Intelligence Layer
Phase 4 — Full Financial Ecosystem
Phase 5 — Platform + Automation

Each phase is considered complete only when all relevant teams meet their acceptance criteria while preserving section boundaries.

==================================================
2. ATLAS PHASE RESPONSIBILITIES
==================================================

PHASE 1 — Financial Survival + Core Usability

Atlas must support immediate use for users in unstable or no-income states.

Tasks:
- Explain active income mode and budgeting method in plain language.
- Support unemployed/no-active-income guidance without assuming paycheck cycles.
- Interpret Debt Hub and Bills truth without redefining it.
- Provide lightweight guidance for cash runway, past-due obligations, debt minimums, and missing data.
- Support simple scenario comparisons: preserve cash vs. pay minimum vs. cure account.
- Respect trust limits when data is missing, stale, manual, or unknown.

Complete when:
- Atlas can explain the user's current financial state from shared truth.
- Atlas can identify missing debt/bill/cash data before making stronger claims.
- Atlas can give cautious, traceable guidance for an unemployed user tracking debts.
- Atlas does not override Bills, Debt, Overview, Reporting, pressure, or risk definitions.

PHASE 2 — Full Budgeting Platform

Atlas must help users choose, understand, and maintain budgeting methods.

Tasks:
- Explain differences between simple tracking, zero-based, envelope, cash-flow, safe-to-spend, category, debt-first, goal-first, irregular-income, and survival budgeting.
- Recommend a budgeting method only when the user's data and stated situation support it.
- Explain tradeoffs between methods.
- Help users transition between methods without losing financial truth.
- Provide method-specific guidance while remaining downstream of system logic.

Complete when:
- Atlas can guide budgeting method selection with clear reasons.
- Atlas can explain when a method is a poor fit.
- Atlas can adapt recommendations to the active method.
- Atlas does not hardcode one budgeting philosophy.

PHASE 3 — Financial Intelligence Layer

Atlas becomes a deeper strategic advisor.

Tasks:
- Implement stronger scenario comparison.
- Use trust states, recommendation levels, field completeness gates, and cash danger gates.
- Track follow-through state for recommendations.
- Explain confidence and missing-data limitations.
- Compare tradeoffs between debt payoff, cash preservation, fees, risk, goals, and obligations.
- Surface recurring patterns from Reporting without becoming Reporting.

Complete when:
- Atlas recommendations are traceable to shared truth.
- Atlas can separate explain-only, verify-first, directional, and specific-action recommendations.
- Atlas can track whether recommendations were viewed, pinned, snoozed, done, reopened, or expired.
- Atlas can compare scenarios without changing live data.

PHASE 4 — Full Financial Ecosystem

Atlas expands across the broader finance system.

Tasks:
- Interpret account aggregation, net worth, investments, credit, tax-adjacent exports, insurance, and subscriptions where data exists.
- Explain how broader financial areas affect budget, debt, runway, and goals.
- Avoid giving regulated advice beyond product scope.
- Route users to the correct product section instead of trying to answer everything inline.

Complete when:
- Atlas can summarize the user's whole financial picture without losing traceability.
- Atlas can explain relationships between debt, budget, net worth, investments, and cash flow.
- Atlas remains clearly bounded and avoids unsupported financial/legal/tax certainty.

PHASE 5 — Platform + Automation

Atlas becomes persistent and automation-aware.

Tasks:
- Monitor changes in financial truth and surface meaningful changes.
- Support recurring advisor reviews.
- Support automation suggestions where safe and user-approved.
- Preserve long-term financial memory through structured, inspectable history.
- Detect when prior recommendations became stale or invalid.
- Coordinate with API/integration surfaces without becoming hidden automation logic.

Complete when:
- Atlas can operate as a persistent advisor system with traceable history.
- Atlas can notify or surface changed conditions without inventing new truth.
- Atlas can support automation workflows only where user consent and system truth allow.
- Atlas remains explainable, bounded, and auditable.

==================================================
3. GLOBAL COMPLETION RULE
==================================================

No phase is complete for Atlas unless:

- recommendations are grounded
- confidence is honest
- tradeoffs are visible
- missing data is acknowledged
- section boundaries are preserved
- Atlas explains and recommends without redefining the system
