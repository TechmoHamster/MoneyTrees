Subject: Debt Section Introduction and Bills Integration Next Steps

Bills Team —

Debt is now an approved product section within MoneyTrees.

Debt introduces a new structured source of debt truth that must integrate cleanly with the Bills operational workspace.

==================================================
1. BILLS ROLE REMINDER
==================================================

Bills is the operational data layer.

Bills exists to:
- capture bill data cleanly
- maintain the canonical bills workspace
- support table-first management
- preserve bill truth and payment records

Bills is not:
- a recommendation layer
- a prioritization layer
- a debt-management clone

==================================================
2. CORE INTEGRATION PRINCIPLE
==================================================

Debt owns:
- debt account structure
- debt math
- debt schedules
- debt states

Bills owns:
- operational bill rows
- payment capture
- payment record truth

Debt must feed Bills.
Bills must not become Debt.

==================================================
3. WHAT BILLS SHOULD EXPECT FROM DEBT
==================================================

Debt will generate:
- upcoming payment obligations
- installment schedules
- loan payment schedules

Only near-term obligations should flow into Bills.

==================================================
4. BILLS NEXT STEPS AFTER CODEX OUTPUT
==================================================

1. Review Debt integration output
- confirm structure of incoming debt-linked obligations
- validate field mapping and row structure

2. Implement debt-linked bill rows
- represent debt obligations as standard operational rows
- maintain clear linkage to the source debt account

3. Preserve table-first integrity
- do not overload the table with full lifetime schedules
- limit visible entries to near-term obligations
- keep scanning and grouping behavior fast

4. Maintain payment capture flow
- payments recorded in Bills remain canonical
- do not move payment capture into Debt

==================================================
5. NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES
==================================================

- Debt must not turn Bills into a debt-management UI
- Bills must not expose debt account editing
- no duplicate truth between Bills and Debt
- no hidden recalculations in bill rows

==================================================
6. ESCALATION CONDITIONS
==================================================

Flag immediately if:
- debt schedules flood the Bills table
- Bills starts owning debt logic
- payment capture becomes ambiguous
- duplicate sources of truth appear
- table performance or clarity degrades

==================================================
7. WORKING EXPECTATION
==================================================

Bills remains the action center.

Debt will provide structured obligations.
Bills will operationalize them cleanly.
