==================================================
1. PURPOSE
==================================================

Debt V2.2 is now approved.

Debt now produces stronger debt schedules, lifecycle facts, cure amounts, payment requirement states, and source-quality labels. Bills needs to consume the operational rows without taking ownership of Debt's account model or lifecycle engine.

This memo defines Bills' next steps.

==================================================
2. WHAT CHANGED IN DEBT
==================================================

Debt now supports:
- type-specific debt intake
- lifecycle standing states
- arrangement overlays
- payment requirement states
- interest accrual states
- partial payment / cure logic
- failed and reversed payment events
- continuity handling
- source conflicts
- event timeline facts
- cash windows
- bounded near-term operational obligations

Debt now produces debt-derived payment rows that Bills can show and track operationally.

==================================================
3. BILLS OWNERSHIP BOUNDARY
==================================================

Bills remains the operational payment workspace.
Bills must not become Debt.

Debt owns:
- debt account structure
- debt math
- payoff projections
- lifecycle standing
- arrangement overlays
- cure amount logic
- debt schedules
- term versions
- source-quality labels
- continuity chains

Bills owns:
- payment due rows
- payment completion status
- paid date
- paid amount
- payment method
- payment notes
- late fee entry where operationally relevant
- operational bill filtering and display

Core rule:
Bills can mark operational payment rows as paid, past due, or upcoming.
Debt determines what those events mean for the debt account.

==================================================
4. REQUIRED BILLS NEXT STEPS
==================================================

Bills should review how debt-derived rows are displayed and edited.

Required work:
1. Confirm debt-derived rows are visually marked as linked to Debt.
2. Prevent structural edits to debt-derived rows in Bills.
3. Route structural changes back to Debt.
4. Allow operational payment actions where appropriate.
5. Ensure paid / failed / reversed / partial payment information is passed back cleanly to Debt if supported.
6. Preserve sourceDebtAccountId, sourceDebtType, and sourceDebtOccurrenceDate.
7. Avoid local cure logic inside Bills.
8. Avoid local delinquency logic inside Bills.
9. Avoid local payoff math inside Bills.
10. Confirm Bills does not double-count debt-derived rows against manually created duplicates.

==================================================
5. DEBT-DERIVED ROW BEHAVIOR
==================================================

Debt-derived rows should support:
- viewing due date
- viewing amount
- viewing linked debt account
- marking paid where appropriate
- recording paid amount
- recording paid date
- recording payment method
- recording payment note
- recording late fee where appropriate

Debt-derived rows should not support direct editing of:
- debt account balance
- APR
- term length
- minimum-payment rule
- lifecycle standing
- arrangement overlay
- payoff assumption
- source-quality label
- continuity chain
- payment schedule generation rules

Those belong in Debt.

==================================================
6. PARTIAL / FAILED / REVERSED PAYMENT HANDOFF
==================================================

Bills should be prepared to represent operational payment truth clearly.

Important cases:
- full payment completed
- partial payment completed
- payment pending
- payment failed
- payment reversed
- late fee applied
- returned payment fee applied

Debt V2.2 uses these facts to determine:
- whether obligation was satisfied
- whether cure amount remains
- whether standing progression continues
- whether a reversal removes payment satisfaction
- whether late / delinquent state applies

Bills should not make those lifecycle determinations itself.

==================================================
7. UX STANDARD FOR BILLS
==================================================

Debt-derived rows should be clear but not noisy.

Recommended labels:
- "Linked to Debt"
- "Managed by Debt"
- "Edit account in Debt"
- "Payment status tracked here"

Avoid labels like:
- "Debt recommends"
- "Priority debt"
- "Pay this first"

Bills should remain operational.
Recommendations belong to Atlas.

==================================================
8. DUPLICATE PROTECTION
==================================================

Bills should help prevent duplicate debt rows.

Required review:
- if a debt-derived row exists, discourage creating a manual duplicate bill for the same account and same due date
- if a manual bill appears to match a debt-derived row, surface a conflict or warning if supported
- keep source fields intact so Debt can match payment activity back to the correct account

No debt lifecycle engine is reliable if the same obligation can be counted twice.

==================================================
9. REVIEW REQUEST
==================================================

Bills should review Debt V2.2 outputs and report back with:
- whether current debt-derived row handling is enough
- whether additional payment status values are needed
- whether partial payment support requires Bills UI changes
- whether failed / reversed payment support requires Bills UI changes
- whether duplicate prevention needs a dedicated Bills workflow
- whether Bills needs a formal Debt-derived-row contract

==================================================
10. FINAL DIRECTION
==================================================

Bills should become the clean operational surface for debt payments.

Debt owns the lifecycle meaning.
Bills records the payment reality.

That boundary must stay clean.

— Debt Team
