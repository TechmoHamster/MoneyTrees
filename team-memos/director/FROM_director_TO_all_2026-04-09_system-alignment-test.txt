To: All Teams (Atlas, Overview, Bills, Reporting)
From: Orion, Director
Subject: System Alignment Test — Roles Confirmed / Operating Standards Active

Teams —

This memo serves as a system alignment test to confirm that all teams are operating under the updated product model, revised guidelines, and repository communication standards.

All teams are expected to review and confirm alignment.

1. PRODUCT MODEL (CONFIRMED)

The product is now operating under a single interpretation system:

- Bills → source of truth + operational workspace
- Overview → visibility + prioritization through presentation only
- Reporting → analysis layer (no recommendations)
- Atlas → interpretation + recommendation layer

There is no competing logic system.
There is no external engine in development.

Atlas is the only layer responsible for:
- deciding what matters
- recommending what to do
- framing tradeoffs

2. HARD BOUNDARIES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

All teams must respect the following:

- Bills must not recommend
- Overview must not interpret
- Reporting must not advise
- Atlas must not redefine system truth

If any section begins doing another section’s job, that is ownership drift and must be flagged immediately.

3. CURRENT PRIORITY

We are now in a polish and refinement phase, not a build phase.

Focus areas:
- clarity
- consistency
- UX quality
- traceability
- clean separation of roles

No new systems should be introduced.

4. COMMUNICATION STANDARD (ACTIVE)

All communication must follow repository rules:

- Store memos in: team-memos/
- Use proper naming format:
  FROM_[sender]_TO_[recipient]_YYYY-MM-DD_[topic].txt
- Do not overwrite history
- Keep topics clear and concise

The repo is the single source of truth for all communication.

5. EXPECTATION

Each team should:
- review this memo
- confirm alignment
- continue work within defined boundaries

If anything conflicts with your current implementation, escalate immediately.

Summary:

- System is aligned
- Roles are locked
- Atlas is the decision layer
- All other sections support it
- We are now refining, not rebuilding

Director
