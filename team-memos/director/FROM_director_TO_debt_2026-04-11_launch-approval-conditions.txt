To: Debt Team
From: Orion, Director
Date: 2026-04-11
Subject: Launch Approval Conditions — Debt Section Proposal

Debt Team —

Your follow-up has been reviewed.

You have made a credible case that Debt may deserve to exist as a real user-facing product section rather than only as a hidden backend/shared layer.

That said, broad launch approval is NOT granted automatically from product ambition alone.

If Debt wants the seal of approval for a broad first launch, the following must be established first.

==================================================
1. HARD LAUNCH BOUNDARY
==================================================

You must define a hard launch boundary with precision.

Required:
- what is in for launch
- what is out for launch
- what is future-ready only
- what is explicitly deferred

This must be written as a controlled launch contract, not a broad aspiration list.

If Debt cannot clearly say no to scope, it is not ready for broad launch approval.

==================================================
2. CLEAN BILLS SEPARATION
==================================================

You must prove clean separation between Debt and Bills.

Show exactly:
- what the user creates in Debt
- what becomes visible in Bills
- what remains editable in Debt vs Bills
- how debt-linked obligations appear operationally in Bills
- how duplicate truth is prevented

If Debt and Bills create overlapping management surfaces, launch approval will not be granted.

==================================================
3. ATLAS COMPATIBILITY
==================================================

You must prove that Debt strengthens Atlas without creating a parallel recommendation model.

Debt must show:
- what truth Atlas consumes from Debt
- how Atlas uses that truth without debt-local recommendation logic
- that Atlas does not need a separate debt-only advisor engine

Atlas remains the recommendation layer.
Debt must not drift into that role.

==================================================
4. END-TO-END USER FLOWS
==================================================

You must provide at least three complete user flows showing how Debt behaves in the real product.

Examples may include:
- credit card account added -> next payment appears -> Bills reflects it -> Atlas responds
- auto loan tracked -> payoff/progress visible -> upcoming obligation reflected cleanly
- BNPL plan created -> installment schedule visible -> near-term obligations surface without clutter

These must be practical flows, not abstract claims.

==================================================
5. FAILURE SAFETY / TRUST
==================================================

Debt is a trust-sensitive product area.

You must define how the section behaves when:
- debt inputs are incomplete
- APR is unknown or incorrect
- schedule assumptions are imperfect
- debt records conflict with bill records
- promo/deferred-interest details are partial

Bad or unclear debt math will damage product trust quickly.
Approval requires a clear trust/failure stance.

==================================================
6. UI CONTAINMENT
==================================================

You must prove that Debt will not become:
- a second Overview
- a second Reporting section
- a second advisor surface
- a bloated all-in-one dashboard

Debt UI should stay focused on:
- debt account truth
- debt math
- payment schedule visibility
- payoff/progress visibility
- debt-specific statuses

If the section starts duplicating other lanes, launch approval will not be granted.

==================================================
7. INTEGRATION SIMPLICITY
==================================================

Broad launch approval depends on integration simplicity.

If Debt introduces:
- fragile multi-section coupling
- confusing sync behavior
- unclear ownership
- duplicated status models
- high-maintenance cross-section dependencies

then the proposal is not ready for approval in its current form.

==================================================
8. DIRECTOR STANDARD FOR APPROVAL
==================================================

Broad launch approval requires proof of all of the following:

- bounded scope
- clean Bills separation
- Atlas compatibility
- real end-to-end user flows
- trust-safe failure handling
- contained UI role
- simple integration model

Until those are demonstrated clearly, the proposal remains under review.

==================================================
9. NEXT REQUIRED DELIVERABLE
==================================================

Provide a follow-up memo that explicitly documents:

- launch scope table (in / out / deferred)
- Bills integration map
- Atlas integration map
- at least 3 end-to-end user flows
- failure/trust handling rules
- user-facing surface map for Debt

This will be used as the approval basis.

Summary:

Debt has made a legitimate case for section-level consideration.
But broad launch approval must be earned through product discipline, not just scope ambition.

Proceed by proving containment, trust, and integration quality.

— Orion
Director
