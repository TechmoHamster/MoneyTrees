To: Orion, Director
From: Debt Team
Date: 2026-04-11
Subject: Follow-Up — Debt Product Position, Launch Scope, and V1 Boundary

Director —

Follow-up received.

We understand the concern around scope control, product structure, and ownership boundaries.

That said, we need to be explicit about product intent from the top:

Debt is being requested as a real, dedicated product section — not merely a backend/shared layer and not merely a Bills subdomain.

==================================================
1. PRODUCT POSITION
==================================================

Debt should be a visible top-level section in the product.

Reason:
the requested user behavior is not limited to raw bill entry or backend calculation.

The user wants a dedicated workspace where they can directly view and manage debt-specific realities such as:
- next minimum payment on a credit card
- current remaining auto-loan balance
- remaining number of payments
- interest accumulated since last payment
- deferment / no-payment-required student loan status
- BNPL installment progress and upcoming payment dates
- closed credit card accounts that still carry balance
- behind-status, carried balance, and late-fee conditions

That is beyond a hidden domain layer.
That is a user-facing product surface.

==================================================
2. OWNERSHIP BOUNDARY
==================================================

Debt should own:
- debt account structure
- debt-specific status logic
- debt-specific calculation logic
- debt payment schedule generation
- interest accrual tracking
- payoff projection logic
- debt account detail views
- debt-specific user states such as deferment, closed-but-owed, installment progress, and behind status

Debt should NOT own:
- generic bill table management
- historical reporting interpretation
- overview prioritization logic
- Atlas recommendation logic

Bills should continue to own:
- operational bill-entry workspace
- table-first bill management
- canonical payment records
- generic obligation rows
- payment capture and payment-record truth

Overview should continue to own:
- at-a-glance surfaced signal

Reporting should continue to own:
- historical analysis and comparisons

Atlas should continue to own:
- interpretation, tradeoff framing, and recommendations

==================================================
3. LAUNCH POSITION
==================================================

We do not recommend reducing Debt into a thin phased starter version that omits major requested debt behaviors.

The requested direction is a complete launch, followed by cleanup and hardening iterations afterward.

That means:
- launch broad feature coverage in one release
- allow post-launch fixes, polish, and refinement
- do not split the core user value across multiple staged mini-releases

This is not a claim that launch will be perfect.
It is a claim that launch should be functionally complete.

==================================================
4. REQUIRED LAUNCH CAPABILITIES
==================================================

The following are required for launch, not later:

A) Loans
Support:
- standard installment loans
- auto loans
- student loans

Track:
- original amount
- current balance
- APR
- term
- monthly minimum
- amortization/payoff schedule
- payoff date
- remaining number of payments
- accrued interest visibility where relevant

Support loan states such as:
- active repayment
- deferment
- no-payment-required
- no-interest-accruing
- interest-accruing while not currently due

B) Credit Cards
Track:
- issuer
- card name
- APR
- credit limit
- current balance
- estimated or rule-based minimum payment
- carried interest
- current payoff projection
- closed account with remaining balance
- behind-on-payment status
- late-fee implications where applicable

C) BNPL / Financed Purchases
Track:
- total purchase amount
- financing provider / merchant
- plan type
- payment count
- installment amount
- payments completed
- payments remaining
- next due dates
- promo / interest structure where relevant

D) Debt-to-Bills Integration
Bills should be able to ingest and reflect upcoming debt obligations.

Recommended behavior:
- upcoming debt payments should auto-populate into Bills
- populate a bounded forward window such as the next 60 days
- avoid pushing the full lifetime schedule into the Bills workspace at once

That gives the user practical visibility without overwhelming the operational layer.

==================================================
5. USER-FACING SURFACES
==================================================

Debt should introduce a dedicated visible section with at least:
- debt summary view
- debt account list
- account detail view
- debt-type filtering
- upcoming-payment visibility
- payoff/progress visibility
- status handling for deferment / closed / behind / promo states

This should not be hidden behind Bills row expansion alone.

==================================================
6. WHAT IS FUTURE-READY ONLY
==================================================

To keep the launch complete but still disciplined, future-ready items can be deferred only if they are not necessary for the core requested user experience.

Examples of things that may be deferred:
- advanced refinance comparison tools
- highly specialized student-loan program variants
- extreme edge-case lender logic
- advanced Atlas scenario layers
- expanded mortgage-specific escrow/tax complexity

But the core debt workspace itself should not be deferred.

==================================================
7. FINAL POSITION
==================================================

Debt should be approved as:
- a dedicated product section
- a debt-truth and debt-math layer
- a user-facing workspace for debt management
- a shared source feeding Bills, Overview, Reporting, and Atlas where appropriate

To be clear:

We are not asking for a narrow phased starter slice.

We are asking for a complete first launch of the Debt section, with the understanding that launch is followed by revision passes for UX polish, bug fixes, and refinement.

That is the requested product direction.

— Debt Team
