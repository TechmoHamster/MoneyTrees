Subject: V2C Review Concerns to Carry Forward

Debt Team —

V2C is approved for progression, but the following concerns should be preserved as internal review notes and carried into later refinement work.

==================================================
1. THRESHOLD-BASED FLAGS ARE CURRENTLY HARD-CODED
==================================================

The V2C factual flag layer is useful and appropriately bounded.

However, several flags are currently driven by fixed product constants.

Examples:
- promo expiring soon = 30 days
- high utilization = 80%
- due soon = 14 days
- payment due soon = 7 days
- timing cluster gap = 7 days

Why this matters:
- these thresholds are reasonable for V2C
- but they are still product-policy choices
- future product needs may require user-level, account-level, or app-level configurability
- downstream teams may interpret these flags as fixed truth when they are actually policy thresholds

Carry-forward note:
Future refinement should consider centralizing these thresholds as explicit product policy and eventually making some of them configurable if that fits the app direction.

==================================================
2. CONSEQUENCE VISIBILITY DEPENDS HEAVILY ON MANUAL ACCOUNT TRUTH
==================================================

The consequence layer is factual and boundary-correct.

However, several consequence outputs depend on the user entering and maintaining specific fields correctly.

Examples:
- promo expiration risk requires promo balance and promo end date
- grace-period loss requires the grace-period status field
- delinquency progression requires days past due
- late fee exposure requires late fee amount

Why this matters:
- the system is only as accurate as the debt truth entered by the user
- weak or stale inputs can hide relevant consequences
- the app may need stronger reminders or missing-data prompts later

Carry-forward note:
Future refinement should keep making manual-data dependency visible and may need additional input-quality prompts, stale-data indicators, or verification flows.

==================================================
3. MINIMUM CASH NEEDED TO STAY CURRENT MAY DOUBLE-COUNT IN EDGE CASES
==================================================

The cash-window system correctly adds near-term required payments and recorded past-due catch-up pressure.

Potential issue:
In some account states, a past-due amount may already be represented as a past-due debt-derived bill row. If so, adding both the past-due row total and the account-level past-due amount could inflate the minimum cash needed to stay current.

Why this matters:
- the current approach is directionally useful
- but edge cases may overstate short-term cash need
- this is especially relevant when Bills preserves historical / past-due debt rows and Debt also stores account-level past-due amount

Carry-forward note:
Future refinement should verify whether account-level past-due amount and past-due schedule rows are mutually exclusive, overlapping, or intentionally additive. If overlap is possible, add guardrails to avoid double-counting.

==================================================
4. BOTTOM LINE
==================================================

V2C remains approved.

These concerns do not block progression to V2D.
They are internal Debt review notes that should be preserved for later refinement and post-phase cleanup.

— Debt Team
