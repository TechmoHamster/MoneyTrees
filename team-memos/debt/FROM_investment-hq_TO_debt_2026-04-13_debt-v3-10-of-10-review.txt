SUBJECT: Debt V3 Review — Current Assessment and 10/10 Requirements

==================================================
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
==================================================

Debt V3 is directionally strong and materially aligned with the Investment HQ mission.

Current Investment HQ score: 8.7 / 10

The V3 scope correctly treats the next product push as a maturity push rather than a reinvention. That is the right strategic direction. V1 made Debt real. V2 is making Debt decision-grade. V3 should make Debt mature, dependable, and robust enough that future work becomes incremental rather than another broad product leap.

The strongest parts of the current V3 scope are:
- accuracy improvement
- verification workflows
- trust and confidence signaling
- credit-card maturity
- real-world debt edge-case handling
- restrained automation
- downstream leverage without expanding Debt's product ownership

The main reason V3 does not yet score 10/10 is that the scope currently reads more like a strong maturity thesis than a hard final-product standard.

To reach 10/10, V3 needs measurable acceptance criteria, stronger failure-state handling, clearer debt-health integrity checks, and sharper factual outputs that support downstream financial decision-making without turning Debt into an advisor or strategy engine.

==================================================
2. INVESTMENT HQ MISSION FIT
==================================================

Debt V3 strongly supports the Investment HQ mission.

Investment HQ prioritizes:
- data over opinion
- evidence over narrative
- probability over certainty
- risk before reward
- long-term durability over short-term convenience

Debt V3 aligns with this because it focuses on debt truth, debt math, statement verification, stale-data visibility, mismatch detection, inspectable calculations, and accurate treatment of messy debt realities.

This is exactly the kind of foundation required for rational financial decision-making. A financial system cannot make good recommendations, projections, or risk assessments if the upstream debt data is weak.

Debt V3 should therefore be treated as a financial-truth infrastructure layer.

It does not need to become a strategy engine. In fact, it should not. But it must produce debt facts with enough accuracy, transparency, and context that Atlas, Overview, Reporting, Bills, and future decision-support surfaces can safely consume them.

Assessment:
- Mission fit: Strong
- Mission exceedance: Partial
- Reason: V3 exceeds normal expectations for debt-data trust and verification, but it is still infrastructure, not the full decision system.

==================================================

Debt V3 aligns well with proven financial-health practices.

Strong financial debt management depends on:
- knowing exactly what is owed
- knowing what is due and when
- avoiding missed payments
- understanding interest behavior
- understanding credit-card minimum-payment mechanics
- understanding promotional and deferred-interest risk
- maintaining accurate records
- using structured payoff decisions rather than monthly improvisation

Debt V3 supports these practices by improving:
- minimum payment realism
- payoff accuracy
- credit-card modeling
- statement verification
- stale-data warnings
- mismatch detection
- confidence labeling
- real-world account-state handling

The missing piece is not philosophy. The missing piece is operational completeness.

Good debt health is not just accurate tracking. It is accurate tracking plus enough factual pressure signals to help the user or downstream product understand what needs attention.

Debt does not need to recommend a strategy directly, but it should expose clean facts such as:
- minimum cash required to stay current
- past-due exposure
- near-term debt cash pressure
- promo expiration risk
- account freshness risk
- confidence level of each projection

==================================================
6. WHAT V3 NEEDS TO REACH 10/10
==================================================

To score 10/10 under the Investment HQ lens, Debt V3 needs the following additions.

--------------------------------------------------
A. HARD V3 ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
--------------------------------------------------

V3 needs a pass/fail checklist that defines when the product is actually mature.

Recommended acceptance criteria:
- Every supported debt type has documented modeling rules.
- Every projection shows its assumptions.
- Every account has a confidence / trust state.
- Every trust state has a defined reason.
- Every stale account is visibly flagged.
- Every manual or estimated field is identifiable.
- Every statement-derived field stores last verified date.
- Every credit-card rule can be verified, marked stale, marked limited, or marked manual.
- Every downstream output has an owner, definition, and freshness status.
- Every low-data state degrades gracefully instead of pretending to be precise.

Without this, V3 risks being impressive but still subjective.

--------------------------------------------------
B. DEBT HEALTH INTEGRITY CHECKS
--------------------------------------------------

V3 should include internal checks that ask whether the debt math still makes sense.

Examples:
- Balance changed unexpectedly.
- Minimum due changed outside expected tolerance.
- Payment posted but projection did not update.
- Statement balance and current balance diverge in a way that affects projections.
- Promo balance is close to expiration.
- Account is marked current but has stale verification.
- Closed account still has remaining balance.
- Manual override conflicts with statement-derived logic.

These checks are not recommendations. They are data-integrity and financial-risk flags.

--------------------------------------------------
C. STRONGER FAILURE-STATE HANDLING
--------------------------------------------------

V3 needs to define what happens when real life breaks the clean model.

Required failure states:
- statement missing
- lender rule changed
- user skipped updates
- balance unavailable
- minimum payment unavailable
- promo terms incomplete
- account is paused / hardship / deferred
- payment posted late
- account closed but still owed
- calculation confidence is too weak for reliable projection

Each failure state should answer:
- What is known?
- What is unknown?
- What calculations are still safe?
- What outputs should be downgraded?
- What warning should the user see?
- What downstream systems should be told?

This is one of the clearest ways to make V3 feel genuinely mature.

--------------------------------------------------
D. CASH-FLOW PRESSURE SIGNALS
--------------------------------------------------

Debt should not become Atlas, but it should expose factual cash-pressure outputs.

Recommended outputs:
- debt cash required in next 7 days
- debt cash required in next 14 days
- debt cash required in next 30 days
- debt cash required in next 60 days
- minimum needed to stay current
- past-due amount
- delinquency-risk exposure
- promo-expiration amount and date
- deferred-interest exposure
- accounts requiring verification

These are not strategic recommendations. They are facts. But they are extremely useful facts for financial health.

--------------------------------------------------
E. SOURCE AUDIT TRAIL
--------------------------------------------------

Every major debt output should be traceable.

Recommended source labels:
- manual entry
- statement-derived
- lender rule
- calculated
- estimated
- custom override
- stale
- limited-data
- imported
- user-confirmed

For each important output, the user should be able to answer:
- Where did this number come from?
- When was it last verified?
- How confident is the system?
- What assumption is being used?
- What would make this number wrong?

This directly supports the Investment HQ standard of evidence over narrative.

--------------------------------------------------
F. CREDIT-CARD MODELING STANDARD
--------------------------------------------------

Credit cards should receive a dedicated maturity checklist because they are the highest-risk debt type.

Recommended requirements:
- separate statement balance, current balance, and payoff balance where applicable
- support for minimum payment formulas by rule type
- minimum-payment verification against statements
- purchase APR / cash advance APR / promo APR awareness where supported
- balance bucket handling where practical
- promo expiration date tracking
- deferred-interest risk tracking
- residual / trailing interest warning support
- payment allocation assumptions clearly stated
- distinction between actual minimum due and user payment strategy
- stale issuer-rule detection

The goal is not perfect issuer replication in every case. The goal is honest fidelity, visible assumptions, and clear degradation when precision is not possible.

--------------------------------------------------
G. DOWNSTREAM CONTRACTS
--------------------------------------------------

Debt V3 should define clean output contracts for the rest of MoneyTrees.

Recommended downstream outputs:
- account balance truth
- minimum due truth
- due date truth
- debt cash-pressure windows
- payoff projection confidence
- stale-data flags
- promo-risk flags
- past-due flags
- closed-but-owed flags
- debt-type-specific risk flags

Each output should define:
- source
- freshness
- confidence
- owner
- intended consumer
- whether it is safe for recommendations

This keeps Debt from becoming Atlas while still making Atlas, Overview, Reporting, and Bills stronger.

--------------------------------------------------
H. MAINTENANCE WORKFLOW MATURITY
--------------------------------------------------

V3 should reduce manual upkeep without hiding logic.

Recommended workflows:
- statement refresh checklist
- stale account review queue
- mismatch review queue
- promo deadline review queue
- accounts needing user confirmation
- accounts with fragile projections
- rules requiring re-verification

The key principle:
Automation may assist, but the system must remain inspectable.

--------------------------------------------------
I. DEFINITION OF “NO V4 NEEDED”
--------------------------------------------------

If V3 is intended to be the final evolutionary leap, the team should explicitly define what that means.

Recommended standard:
Debt V3 is complete when future improvements are mostly:
- issuer coverage improvements
- automation refinements
- UX polish
- bug fixes
- additional edge-case support
- performance/stability improvements
- minor modeling refinements

Debt V3 is not complete if future work still requires:
- redefining Debt's role
- rebuilding trust states
- rebuilding credit-card modeling
- rebuilding verification workflows
- changing downstream ownership boundaries
- adding basic failure-state handling after the fact

==================================================
7. STRATEGIC WARNING
==================================================

The biggest risk for V3 is mistaking a comprehensive scope for a complete product standard.

The current direction is strong. But a 10/10 maturity push requires more than broad goals. It needs explicit operational tests.

The Debt team should be able to say:
- This account is accurate enough because X.
- This projection is limited because Y.
- This output is stale because Z.
- This recommendation surface can safely use this signal.
- This calculation should not be trusted beyond this boundary.

That is the difference between a good personal-finance feature and a serious financial decision-support system.

Proceed with Debt V3, but tighten it before implementation.

Convert the current V3 scope into a formal V3 Product Acceptance Standard.

That standard should include:
- trust-state rules
- debt-type modeling requirements
- credit-card maturity requirements
- failure-state behavior
- verification workflows
- stale-data handling

- cash-pressure factual signals

If those are added, Debt V3 can reasonably move from 8.7 / 10 to 10 / 10 under the Investment HQ review standard.

Bottom line:
Debt V3 is already strategically strong. To become a true full-stop maturity release, it needs to become testable, auditable, and operationally complete.
