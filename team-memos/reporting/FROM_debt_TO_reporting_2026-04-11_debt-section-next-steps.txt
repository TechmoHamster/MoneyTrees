Subject: Debt Section Introduction and Reporting Next Steps

Reporting Team —

Debt is now an approved product section within MoneyTrees.

Debt introduces a new source of structured financial truth focused on debt accounts, balances, schedules, and states.

==================================================
1. REPORTING ROLE REMINDER
==================================================

Reporting is the analysis layer.

Reporting exists to:
- explain what changed
- compare periods
- show trends and patterns
- provide structured analytical reads

Reporting is not:
- a recommendation layer
- a prioritization layer
- a decision engine

==================================================
2. WHAT REPORTING SHOULD EXPECT FROM DEBT
==================================================

Debt will provide:
- debt balances over time
- payment schedules
- debt state transitions
- utilization signals
- interest visibility where applicable

These become new inputs for analysis.

==================================================
3. REPORTING NEXT STEPS AFTER CODEX OUTPUT
==================================================

1. Review Debt outputs
- confirm data availability
- validate time-based consistency

2. Integrate Debt into reporting models
- include debt in total obligation analysis
- incorporate debt trends across time windows
- include debt-related distributions and concentration views

3. Maintain analytical integrity
- describe what changed
- compare to prior periods
- explain why it matters analytically

4. Keep outputs neutral
- no advice
- no next-step language
- no prioritization framing

==================================================
4. REPORTING GUARDRAILS
==================================================

When integrating Debt:
- do not recommend actions
- do not rank priorities
- do not create local scoring systems
- do not reinterpret system pressure or risk

Reporting should remain analytical only.

==================================================
5. ESCALATION CONDITIONS
==================================================

Flag immediately if:
- debt data introduces recommendation-style outputs
- reporting begins duplicating Debt detail views
- analytical clarity is lost due to poor integration
- time-range logic becomes inconsistent

==================================================
6. WORKING EXPECTATION
==================================================

Reporting should treat Debt as a new upstream data source.

Use it to:
- improve analysis
- improve trend visibility
- improve comparative insight

Do not convert it into advice.

— Debt Team
